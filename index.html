What roles do I work in?

During my carrier I had the chance to work in different roles. I tried to seperate my expertice into commonly known roles. Let's investigate what I did so far.

In my capacity as a Team Lead or Project Lead, my responsibilities encompass translating requirements into solutions and managing task distribution within the team. I lead by example, aiming to inspire and empower my colleagues. Drawing upon the knowledge I have accumulated over the years, mentoring and guiding are integral aspects of my role.

My work starts where ChatGPT's prowess ends. I thrive on pushing the boundaries of generative AI, collaborating closely with data experts to empower and democratize its applications. From crafting ingenious prompts to developing user-friendly web interfaces for data exploration, I'm at the forefront of transforming ideas into reality. As a pivotal member of the generative AI elite, I craft innovative solutions and architectures aimed at delivering unparalleled value to our clients.

As my main field of education my whole work is circled around working with Data.In various projects I could use algorithms for decision trees, like xgboost or rpart, for clustering like kmeans, dbscan or knn. Often I was confronted with the fact of to complex or incomplete data so feature reduction and data imputation are not new to me.

I started as visualization expert with a focus on storytelling, but without forgetting things like Shinderman's mantra or data-to-ink-ratio. In this role I had the chance to work with tools like Tableau & Power BI but also build custom dashboards completly with HTML/CSS/JS and libaries like d3js. Data exploration was always an important part to communicate data to relevant stakeholders and make them see what treasures their data hide.

In each of my previous roles, data pipelines played a central role. I constructed pipelines using tools like Airflow and Azure Pipelines, interfaced with various REST APIs , and contributed to the design of databases and data marts. Crafting complecated SQL queries is a skill I find easy.

As a Machine Learning Engineer, I worked close with Data Scientist to develop custom packages in R or implement solutions to optimize their working processes. I shaped optimization processes with e.g.: Grid searches to tune hpyerparameters for gaining the last couple percentages in accuracy, recall or specificity. I created guidelines and tutorials for Data Scientists & processes to automize processes like model training, deployment or scoring. A big focus in this role was the implementation of packages for different Rest APIs to connect tools like Jira & Confluence within a Data Science Process in a way that stakeholders are closer to insights.

An exciting challange is always to get the work from developers to stabilize it for reccuring processes. In my role as MLOps Team Lead I could help my team to define these processes. I orchestrate the end-to-end development of machine learning solutions. I ensure that AI models are deployed, monitored, and continuously improved for impactful, data-driven outcomes.

As Cloud Architect I was part in transfroming a compoany from their on premise infrastructure to a mostly SaaS focused setup in Azure. I designed and build an end-to-end plattform for Data Scientists from starting from Data exploration to model deployment. As well as integrating inference in existing processes. Tools that I have recently used in that role are: Azure ML, Azure DevOps, Azure DataLake, Azure Synapse Analytics,Azure OpenAI Studio,Azure Data Factory and Azure Databricks.

In mostly data use cases I worked with various technologies that are part of the full stack tool box. Starting from UI/UX design with bootstrap or react to strong backend development with node.js , docker or kubernetes. SQL databases like Oracle or MSSQL to NoSQL technologies like Spark or mongodb.
